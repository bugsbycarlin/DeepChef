Southwestern Stuffed Peppers
-----
Stuffed peppers with a southwestern spin. I always have leftover stuff that will not fit in the peppers, so I will use the rest, scramble in some eggs and make breakfast burritos for a very tasty breakfast!
-----
4 4.5 oz - chopped green chilies
---
2 cups - frozen corn
---
6 0z - greek yogurt
---
2 tsp - ground cumin
---
1 lb - ground turkey
---
2 cups - monterey jack cheese
---
2 T - olive oil
---
1/2 tsp - pepper
---
8 - red bell peppers
---
1 jar - salsa
---
1 tsp - salt
---
1 - small onion
---
2 cups - white rice
-----
1. Heat oven to 375 degrees.
---
2. Cook rice according to directions.
---
3. Cut peppers in half, clean out the seeds and place, cut-side up, into baking pans, trying to put 8 halved peppers per pan.
---
4. Heat oil in pan and cook ground turkey and onion.
---
5. Stir in corn, chilies, cumin, cooked rice, 1/2 of the cheese, salt and pepper.
---
6. Fill the peppers. Add 1/2 cup of water to each pan of 8 peppers. Cover with foil and bake about 40-45 minutes or until peppers are soft. Sprinkle with remaining cheese and bake uncovered for an additional 5 minutes.
---
7. In a small bowl, wisk together the yogurt with 1/4 cup water.
---
Use the yogurt mixture and salsa as a topping to your peppers for serving.
---
